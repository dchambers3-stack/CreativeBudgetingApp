How to Budget (Dave Ramsey Style)

Budgeting isn’t about limiting your freedom — it's about gaining control.

Zero-Based Budgeting means every dollar has a job. Income minus expenses should equal $0.

1. List Your Income

Write down all sources of monthly income: paychecks, side hustles, or any other money coming in.

2. List All Expenses

Include everything — rent, food, entertainment, debt, savings. Organize them into categories to stay clear.

3. Cover the Four Walls

Take care of essentials first:

  • Food
  • Utilities
  • Shelter (like rent/mortgage)
  • Transportation

4. Assign Every Dollar

Subtract each planned expense from your income until you hit zero. That includes saving and debt payments.

5. Track & Adjust

Life isn’t perfect — if something changes mid-month, adjust your budget. Stay flexible and in control.

6. Repeat Monthly

Do a fresh budget before each new month. Each one is different, so your plan should be too.

“A budget is telling your money where to go instead of wondering where it went.” — Dave Ramsey
